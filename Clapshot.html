 Clapshot

 Ingredients

 

  1 pound Potatoes, cubed and peeled
  3/4 pount Turnips, cubed and peeled
  1/4 pound Carrots, cubed and peeled
  1 tsp Salt
  2 tbsp Butter
  3 tbsp Heavy cream

Food Description

 

Clapshot is a traditional Scottish dish that originated in the Orkney Islands. It is a simple but hearty dish made with mashed potatoes and turnips (also known as swedes or rutabagas) mixed together with butter and seasoning. The name "clapshot" comes from the sound of the wooden spoon hitting the side of the pot as the ingredients are mixed together.

 

Kitchen utensils:

 

Stove Burner

Dutch Oven

Sharp Knifey

Potato Masher

 

Difficulty: Easy

 

Preparation Time: 25 mins

 

Cook Time: 25 mins

 

Servings: 8

 

Step One:

 

Place potatoes, turnips, carrots, and salt into a Dutch oven. Fill with water to cover vegetables and bring to a boil over high heat. Reduce the heat to medium-high and boil until vegetables are tender, 20 to 25 minutes.

 

Step Two:

   

Drain and mash vegetables with a potato masher. Spoon mashed vegetables into a serving dish. Top with butter and drizzle with cream.
