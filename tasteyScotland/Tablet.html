 Scottish Tablet

 Ingredients

 

  175g Unsalted butter
  450g Caster sugar
  75g Sweetend condensed milk
 

Food Description

 

Scottish tablet is a traditional sweet treat that is popular in Scotland.
It is a type of fudge that is made with sugar, condensed milk, butter, and sometimes vanilla.
The texture is similar to that of a crumbly, hard, and sweet candy that melts in your mouth.

 

Kitchen utensils:

 

Stove Burner

Pot/Saucepan

Baking Tray

Wooden Cooking Spoon

 

Difficulty: Easy

 

Preparation Time: 5 mins

 

Cook Time: 25 mins

 

Servings: 20-25 pieces

 

Step One:

 

Line a 22 x 18cm tray with baking parchment. Put the butter and 150ml water in a pan over a low heat,
and melt the butter (don't let the water boil). Tip in the sugar and dissolve, stirring often..

 

Step Two:

   

Bring the water to the boil, turn down to a simmer, then add the condensed milk. The mixture will be a light creamy colour.

 

Step Three:

   

Cook for 20-30 mins, stirring often, until the mixture is thick and a deep caramel colour.

 

Step Four:

   

To test, drop a little mixture onto a cold side plate, it should not run and should be thick and sticky after 30 seconds.
Remove from the heat and beat for 10-15 mins until it has slightly thickened.
Pour into the prepared tin and leave to cool for 30 mins before cutting into squares.
